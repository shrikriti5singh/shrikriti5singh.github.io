Consumption and Conseqeunces of Alcohol in England

Intro

At all times in almost any time period, alcohol has been consumed to relax, socialize, and/or celebrate. So much so that it has become a part of many social and cultural practices. But do the consumers realize the other side of alcohol? This data story will try to visualize the side which shows the consumption pattern in the UK, the number of lives it has claimed, and the suffering endured as a result. According to WHO, over 200 health conditions are connected to the harmful use of alcohol. These range from road injuries and violence, to cancer and cardiovascular diseases. It is also a causal factor in more than 60 medical conditions.

Expense Breakdown

Since 2017/18 expenditure on all food and drink eaten out has risen by 1.5 per cent. Expenditure on food and non alcoholic beverages eaten out has risen by 2.0 per cent and expenditure on alcoholic drinks consumed out has fallen by 1.9 per cent.

Alcohol related hospital admissions

A "narrow measure" is used here which estimates the number of hospital admissions which are primarily due to alcohol consumption and provides the best indication of trends in alcohol-related hospital admissions. These are admissions where an alcohol-related disease, injury or condition was the primary reason for a hospital admission or an alcohol-related external cause was recorded in a secondary diagnosis field.

Conclusion

With all the data and trends covered, it can be concluded that the alcohol-attributable diseases continue to burden health systems. The story tries to highlight the key features of consumption and the repercussions of alcohol. It shows the adverse effect of pandemic through death rates. It also establishes that the expenditure percentage on alcohol has been increasing with income. The analysis presented here can hopefully give a new perspective to consumers motivating them to act.
